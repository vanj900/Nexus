# Rust Belt — Demo World

**Genre:** Grimdark post-apocalyptic  
**Loop Iteration:** 3  
**Status:** Demo complete — suitable for use as a campaign setting or procedural generation seed

---

## Step 1 — World Definition

**Genre and Theme**

Grimdark post-apocalyptic. The theme is managed decline — not collapse, but the slow grinding down of everything that made civilisation worth having. People are not fighting for survival in the immediate sense; they are fighting to stay human in a world that is quietly convincing them not to bother.

**Time Period**

Approximately 80 years after the Corrosion Event — a cascading industrial failure that released continent-spanning toxic particulate clouds. The tech level is fragmented: pre-Event industrial technology rusting in place, improvised low-tech adaptations everywhere, and rare salvaged high-tech that confers enormous power on whoever holds it.

**History and Current State**

The Rust Belt was once a manufacturing heartland — foundries, rail networks, river trade, dense working-class cities. The Corrosion Event destroyed most of the infrastructure over 15 years of slow atmospheric contamination. What remains is a patchwork of fortified settlements, scavenger gangs, trade convoys, and isolated communities that have developed radically different coping strategies.

The world is in managed decline. It is not getting better. The question is how fast it is getting worse, and who profits from the pace.

**Major Recent Events (Last 100 Years)**

- **The Corrosion Event (Year 0):** Industrial cascade failure releases toxic particulate. Lung damage becomes endemic within a decade.
- **The First Plague (Year 12):** Respiratory illness kills approximately 30% of the remaining population.
- **The Compact Wars (Years 20–35):** Resource wars over remaining clean water and medicine production. Three major factions emerged: the Syndicate, the Church of the Intact, and the Freeholders.
- **The Long Quiet (Years 35–65):** A cold equilibrium. Population stabilised. Trade networks re-established. Brutal but functional.
- **The Salt Problem (Year 68–present):** The primary river system is now too saline for agriculture near the delta. Communities dependent on river farming are failing. A new conflict is building.

**Primary Sources of Conflict**

- Water rights (the Salt Problem is accelerating)
- Medicine production and distribution (the Syndicate monopoly)
- Clean air — some zones are more contaminated than others
- Ideology: the Church of the Intact believes pre-Event human life should be restored exactly; the Freeholders believe adaptation and mutation are the path forward

**What's Unique**

The apocalypse happened in slow motion and is still happening. Nobody living remembers the before-times firsthand — they're all 3rd-generation post-Event. The nostalgia is inherited, not personal, which makes it both more and less reliable. The world is not fighting to rebuild; it's arguing about whether rebuilding was ever the right goal.

**Average Daily Life**

Most people live in mid-sized settlements of 500–5000 people. They work, they deal with chronic health issues, they distrust strangers from outside their settlement cluster, and they have very specific rituals around resource use. Generosity is performative and rare outside kinship groups. Children are valuable — low birth rates due to health issues.

## Step 2 — Geographical Data

**Setting Shape**

An irregular region roughly 800km × 400km, centred on a major river system (the Runne). The region is bounded to the north by the Ashwall (an area of permanently elevated particulate density from multiple failed industrial sites), to the east by the Spillfields (salt flats and chemical residue, impassable except by specific routes), to the south by the Scrub (thin hardscrabble land, barely farmable, occupied by dispersed Freeholder communities), and to the west by the Greymere (a formerly productive agricultural zone, now partially recovered — controlled by Syndicate agriculture operations).

**Major Biomes**

- **Runne River Corridor:** Moderate contamination, most habitable zone, highest population density
- **Inner Rustland:** Former industrial core, high contamination, rich salvage
- **The Scrub:** Southern semi-arid, low contamination, poor soil
- **Greymere Lowlands:** Recovering farmland, western border, Syndicate-controlled
- **Ashwall Fringe:** Northern approach to the Ashwall — twilight zone of extreme danger and extreme salvage opportunity

**Water Sources**

- Runne River (primary, increasingly saline near delta)
- Aquifers in the Scrub (limited capacity, being drawn down faster than replenishment)
- Cistern systems in fortified settlements (rainwater collection, expensive to maintain)
- The Syndicate's water purification plants at Gorren and Salthead (chokepoint monopoly)

**Natural Resources**

- *Abundant:* Salvage metal, chemical residue with industrial applications, contaminated soil (can be remediated slowly)
- *Scarce:* Clean water, topsoil, uncontaminated air at altitude, viable seed stock, rubber and polymer materials

**Natural Barriers and Chokepoints**

- Ashwall: impassable in any meaningful sense — acts as the northern border
- Spillfields: channels all east-west movement through three passes, all of which are contested
- The Runne crossings: six viable bridges, all controlled by major factions or independent settlement militias

## Step 3 — Flora and Fauna

**Dominant Plants**

Hardy rust-adapted scrub vegetation — low greyish ground cover, some stunted conifers, invasive salt grass near river areas, and the distinctive Ironweed (a tough fibrous plant that grows in contaminated soil and is used for textiles and rope, though it irritates skin).

**Crops**

Dwarf barley (contamination-resistant variety developed over 50 years), contamination-tolerant root vegetables (called "grunts" locally — ugly, earthy, sustaining), mushroom cultivation in sealed settlement interiors, and algae farming at larger water sites.

**Dangerous Plants**

Blister Moss — looks like safe ground cover but secretes a slow-acting toxin that causes neurological symptoms over weeks of exposure. Settlement founders learned about it the hard way. Now a well-known hazard; most children are taught to identify it.

**Fauna**

- *Rustpigs:* Semi-feral descendants of domesticated pigs. Highly contamination-tolerant. Primary meat animal. Bad-tempered and require experienced handlers.
- *Greydeer:* Thin, cautious ungulates adapted to low-nutrient scrub. Hunted, rarely herded.
- *Saltcrows:* Intelligent scavenging birds. Considered omens by some factions. Highly adaptable.
- *Tunnellers:* Rodent species that has thrived post-Event. Grain stores require constant defence.
- *Filterjaw Eels:* Large river-dwelling eels that have adapted to survive in moderate saline conditions. Important food source.

**Apex Predators**

No large megafauna apex predators survived. Humans are the apex. Feral dog packs are the most dangerous wildlife; some have developed disturbing pack cooperation behaviours over three generations of human-pressure selection.

**Symbolic Species**

The Saltcrow is the symbol of the Freeholders — adaptive, scavenging, surviving, despised by those who preferred the old order.

## Step 4 — Environmental Features

**Iconic Landmarks**

- **The Irongate:** A pre-Event industrial complex so large it functions as a small city. Partially habitable. Interior zones of extreme contamination are separated from less-dangerous outer rings by improvised barriers. Controlled by a tenuous factional coalition.
- **The Drowned Yards:** A flooded rail marshalling yard near the river delta. Now a complex of half-submerged rail cars and maintenance buildings used by salvagers and smugglers. Some structures still above water. Treacherous footing.
- **The Spindle:** A pre-Event communications tower that still partially functions. Whoever controls it can communicate across much of the Rust Belt. Currently controlled by a neutral party of communication specialists (the Signal Guild) who sell access to everyone.
- **Gorren:** The largest surviving settlement (~12,000 people). Syndicate-controlled. Has the largest water purification plant. Effective capital of the Syndicate's informal empire.

**Recurring Hazards**

- Particulate storms from the Ashwall — seasonal, give warnings of 2–6 hours. Full-settlement shelter is standard practice.
- Saline flooding in the river delta zone — getting worse every year.
- Salvage collapse events — pre-Event structures failing catastrophically with little warning.

**Zones of Permanent Danger**

- **The Ashwall core:** No human survival beyond 48 hours without sealed suit technology that barely exists. Rich in salvage, which is why people keep dying there.
- **Spillfields interior:** Chemical residue at lethal concentrations. Some salvage at the edges.
- **Irongate Deeps:** The innermost processing cores of the Irongate complex. Unknown contents. Occasional strange sounds. Nobody goes more than twice.

**Ruins**

The entire Rustland interior is ruins. Every city, every factory, every rail hub. The salvage economy depends on these ruins. They also represent an enormous psychological weight — every generation grows up in the wreckage of a civilisation they were told was better than the one they have.

**Environmental Adaptation**

- Filtration masks are standard equipment outside settlements. Quality varies enormously by wealth.
- Most settlements have sealed growing spaces (contamination-controlled).
- Chronic respiratory disease is endemic and normalised. Most people over 40 have some degree of lung damage.

## Step 5 — Infrastructure and Dynamics

### Political

Three major factions have divided most of the Rust Belt into spheres of influence, with significant contested and independent zones:

**The Syndicate**
Corporate successor organisation to the largest pre-Event industrial consortium. Controls water purification at Gorren and Salthead, most of the viable Greymere farmland, and the main river crossings. Legitimacy: contractual — they provide services, you pay. Governance: council of resource managers. Law enforcement: private security forces (Syndicate Wardens).

**The Church of the Intact**
Religious organisation that emerged around a literal belief in restoration — not just physical recovery but the moral and cultural restoration of pre-Event civilisation, which they have romanticised into something it wasn't. Controls several mid-sized settlements in the river corridor. Legitimacy: divine mandate + welfare provision. Law enforcement: the Hands of Order (combination police, clergy, inquisitors).

**The Freeholders**
Loose network of independent communities in the Scrub and fringe zones. No central governance. Shared ideology: adaptation is the path forward, the old world is dead and mourning it is killing people. Legitimacy: community consensus, usually town-meeting style. Law enforcement: community-level; varies from brutal to absent.

**Independent Settlements**
Several important settlements remain genuinely independent, including the Irongate coalition and the Signal Guild at the Spindle.

### Economic

Primary activity: salvage, agriculture, water trade, medicine production. The Syndicate effectively controls a medicine monopoly (they control the manufacturing precursors). Freelance alchemists/chemists produce alternatives of variable quality.

Currency: standardised metal tokens (Syndicate-issued, widely accepted but resented), trade goods in the Freeholder zones, and the Church's system of tithe-and-welfare in their controlled areas.

Trade routes: the Runne River corridor is the primary artery. The three Spillfields passes are secondary routes. The Signal Guild's communication network facilitates trade coordination across factional lines.

### Technological Level

Industrial era, fragmented. Pre-Event machinery is maintained where possible but no longer manufactured. Most technology is adaptive: mechanical improvisation, chemical reprocessing, low-power electrical systems where generators function. No electronics manufacturing. Salvaged electronics from pre-Event are precious and fragile.

The Syndicate has the best technology — they've maintained the most pre-Event infrastructure. Some Freeholder communities have developed effective low-tech innovations that outperform Syndicate solutions in specific domains (water filtration ceramics, contamination-resistant agriculture).

### Social

Clear stratification: Syndicate management at the top, followed by skilled workers, followed by labourers, followed by independent scroungers, followed by the unaffiliated poor. The Church has a parallel hierarchy that cuts across economic lines. Freeholder zones are more egalitarian within communities, brutal toward outsiders.

Social mobility is limited but not impossible — skill in salvage or medicine can raise status. The main mobility path is affiliation with the Syndicate.

**Extinct Careers**

Pre-Event: factory line worker, rail engineer, telecommunications engineer, agricultural scientist (the institution, not the knowledge). Their absence is felt in specific ways — nobody can manufacture new filtration membrane material, so masks get worse over time.

## Step 7 — World Personality

**Core Values**

- *Endurance* — complaining without action is considered a weakness
- *Practical competence* — knowing how to do things is more respected than ancestry or education
- *Group loyalty* — to settlement, faction, or crew before any abstract principle
- *Distrust of rhetoric* — people who talk more than they work are presumed to be running a con

**Taboos**

- Wasting water (public shaming; in some settlements, enforceable by community censure)
- Abandoning a crew member in a contamination zone
- Selling bad medicine knowingly (treated as murder by most communities)
- Claiming to be from a destroyed settlement when you're not (identity fraud in a world where identity is fragile)

**Attitude to Outsiders**

Cold by default. Functional hostility is standard. Trust is extended in stages, based on demonstrated practical value. People who've proven themselves once are remembered; people who've betrayed trust once are not forgiven.

**Handling of Death and Grief**

Pragmatic. Bodies are resources — bones for tools, fat for fuel, organic material for soil enrichment. Grief is acknowledged but brief in public. Extended mourning is considered a luxury. The Church has rituals that allow longer formal grief, which partly explains its appeal.

**Celebration and Ritual**

- The Runne Festival (annual, river corridor): trade fair, music, formal hostility suspension between factions
- Salvage send-offs: communal gathering before a major void-walking attempt; ritual meal, gear check, last words
- Settlement founding anniversaries: remembered intensely; often the only genuine civic pride

**Default Emotional Register**

Flat affect in public, especially in mixed company. Genuine emotion expressed within tight-knit groups or under intoxicants (various local brews of variable safety). Not happy, not in despair — grinding along. The Freeholders are marginally more emotionally expressive; the Church is more performatively solemn.

**Cultural Relationship to History**

Mixed. Pre-Event nostalgia is strong among older populations and the Church. Freeholders explicitly reject pre-Event nostalgia as a form of cognitive contamination — dangerous because it prevents adaptation. Most working-age people have a pragmatic disinterest: the past is dead, what does that tell us about fixing the water?

**Status Markers**

- Quality of filtration mask (the clearest visible class signal)
- Condition of hands (clean hands = Syndicate upper tier; scarred/damaged = worker)
- Weight (heavier = access to better food = higher status; gaunt = scavenger/Void Walker)
- Specific calluses and tool wear patterns (identify career and status within it)

**Humour**

Dark, specific, and technical. Jokes are about death, contamination, and corporate bureaucracy. Absurdist humour is common in Freeholder communities. The Church discourages humour in formal settings; dark humour flourishes in its back rooms anyway.

## Expert Review Notes (Loop 3)

**Psychology:** Trauma from inherited-not-experienced history is underexplored in NPC motivations. Adding 2 NPC types to address this: Church Zealot (nostalgic trauma by proxy) and Freeholder Pragmatist (defensive rejection of grief as coping).

**Sociology:** Salt Problem is generating a new class of internally displaced persons — river delta farmers losing their land. This population has no institutional support and is becoming a recruitment pool for destabilising factions. Added to faction dynamics.

**Behaviour Analysis:** Water waste taboo is stated but the enforcement mechanism isn't tight. Added community water-monitoring roles and specific shaming ritual to the culture section.

**Ecology:** Filterjaw Eel population is being overfished. This will become a food crisis in ~15 years in-world. Added as a slow-burning ecological time bomb.

**History:** The Compact Wars period was under-specified. Sketch of the three key battles and their territorial outcomes added to integration notes.
